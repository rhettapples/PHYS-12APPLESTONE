IDEA: A kinetic sculpture that consists of a hammer that is moved up and down somehow. The hammer would need to be mounted on an axel, and have a cam mounted to a motor that is offset to contact the hammer's bottom and move it up.

First I took measurements of the axel that would be attached to the hammer's bottom

Then I made an outline of what I wanted with a variable as the axel diameter

I chamfered the edges of the hammer and then projected it and exported it as a dxf

I then made cam dimentions that seemed viable, and offset the hole and saved it as a dxc

I made 5 coppies of the cam and the hammer and sent it to the lazer cutter

I glued the hammer pieces, and cam pieces, making sure to keep the holes in line

I tested the motor and started planning out the fitting

After planning out a design I held the motor manually on the hammer to figure out where I should mount it.

I mounted everything and gave it a test run!

It worked perfectly first try thankfully.
