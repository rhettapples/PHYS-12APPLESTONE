My original Idea for a final project was just a grid display that could be operated through a website, I decided this was a bit too boring,

Instead I opted to make a tetris board!

I found a video that did something similar to what I wanted to do by electronoobs, but there were some main changes that I wanted to have: Namely a detached controller, no joystick, no music in the backround, and a few other custom modifications.

Next step was to gather the materials, the largest problem being the 128 leds that were needed to fill the 8x16 board

After getting all the LEDS I found a suitable board to mount them and started cutting and sizing the positioning

Then I started taping them down after I measured the correct placement

This was the final placement of all of them forming the grid, I stuck them down with center rows of tape

After that it was time to start soldering, I had to connect each ground, power and input of each strip to the one above it which took a lot of time to do accurately.

At this point I also started working on the controller, I wanted a pause, start and Dpad of up, down, left, and right

I switched from the larger board to something that would make more sense for size

I taped all the buttons down and began planning

I decided to have pins coming up that I would solder each of the buttons to (and a ground pin)

The first thing to do was solder all of them to a common ground

Then working to solder all of the individual pins for each button, and the common ground

It was at this very moment that I connected the grid and realized something terrible... since the LED strips were very beat up the singals were getting messed up and they couldnt display the correct color. This was a major setback, and I had to get LEDS from microcenter. This also meant that the last 4 hours of work was essentially going down the drain.

I got the new strip and it worked perfectly

It was time to say goodbye and rip out all of the strips and put new ones in, but there was another problem.

As you can see here the spacing on the board is a bit over an inch and a half.

And the only leds at microcenter were about spaced less than an inch. At this point I had two options, make a smaller board, or individually solder 762 connections to make it the right size

I choose the ladder after doing a test strip, knowing it would likely take an entire day.

I cut and lined them up and got to work

This process was extremely extremely tedious, had a high chance of failure if even one solder was messed up, and was the source of much backpain (would not recomend)

I frequently tested to make sure no solders were messed up

It worked! Atleast for the first few rows

This was another test after more rows

And here it was completed in all its glory, It took a full day to get it wired up, I think it was worth it

I put a piece of opaque plastic over it, and let someone give it a test run. Its at this point I should clarify my usage of electronoobs code and library. I took his original file found Here And modified it to take away a lot of the bells and whistles that I didnt like such as the buzzer, joystick, and segmented display, and add a few extra things such as my custom loading screen. I cut down the opaque plastic to match the display

Then I mounted the arduino Mega I was using (was told I could use it) to the back with an on/off switch

I decided to make the lights look more squarelike, so I cut down some foamboard, into strips, also cutting some wires to make the controller easier to hold.

I stripped all the wires on the board, and began to solder

This is what it looked like with all the wires soldered

Then It was time to glue all the rows and colums

I tested it with just the columns to make sure it looked good, then continued with the rows

This process also took a fair amount of time, but I thankfully got help

Did some testruns to make sure it looked good

And this was it with all the rows and colums completed, it looks a lot more tetris-like

I also glued foamboard to the sides to make it look nice

The next thing to do was to design the controller housing which started with measurements of the board, it was drawn up in fusion 360

I extruded the general shape

Added a cap

and added a sidepannel to make it easy to take in and out

I ajusted the orientation so it could print well, and exported it

It was then imported into slic3r and sent to the prusa

It turned out great!
